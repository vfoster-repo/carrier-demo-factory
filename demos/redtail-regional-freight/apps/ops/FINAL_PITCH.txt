Redtail Regional Freight has been running 38 trucks and 52 drivers on Dale Whitfield's memory and a spreadsheet he rebuilds a few times a year — this platform replaces that guesswork with real numbers pulled straight from his own trip, fuel, maintenance, and delivery data. It proves, in dollars, what Dale has suspected but never been able to show: TRK-014 is costing an estimated $31,000+ a year in excess fuel and maintenance versus a normal fleet truck, Llano Estacado Foods' dock is burning nearly 7,000 driver-hours in detention (4.4x every other customer), and three lanes — including the flagship Amarillo-to-Denver reefer route — are netting below breakeven on the majority of their loads once real fuel cost is deducted from the posted rate. The single most valuable insight is the lane profitability page: a route that looks perfectly healthy on a rate confirmation is quietly losing money on 8 of every 10 loads, a fact no rate sheet or spreadsheet was ever going to surface on its own. The daily routine that makes this worth paying for is simple — a two-minute morning check of the Dashboard's priority banner, a Friday cash-flow review before payroll, and a monthly pass through the Truck Retirement and Driver Retention pages — the kind of discipline that catches a $100K problem at $30K instead of discovering it after the fact. Generic trucking software can't do this because it doesn't know Redtail's trucks, customers, or lanes; every number on every page here ties back to an actual load, an actual detention event, or an actual repair ticket from Redtail's own operating history. For $299/month, Redtail would have a standing financial case to retire TRK-014, a defensible number to bring into a Llano Estacado rate conversation, and an early-warning system on cash flow and driver turnover that no longer depends on Dale remembering to rebuild a spreadsheet.
